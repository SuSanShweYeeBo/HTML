Thermage The Latest Skin Tightening Treatment

Thermage is a leading solution for those seeking to rejuvenate their appearance without the invasiveness of surgery. Renowned for its efficacy and safety, Thermage utilizes radiofrequency energy to rejuvenate the skin from within. This energy heats the deeper layers of the skin, causing immediate collagen contraction and encouraging new collagen production over time. The result is a tighter, smoother skin surface and a more youthful appearance.
At Luxe Aesthetics, we’re excited to offer the latest Thermage FLX system. This iteration is equipped with AccuREP technology, which fine-tunes the delivery of radiofrequency energy, ensuring each treatment is optimized for the best possible results.
What sets Thermage FLX apart is not just its technological advancements but also its commitment to improving patient comfort. The system’s design minimizes the sensation of heat, making treatments more comfortable than ever. Additionally, with the FLX model, treatments are quicker, allowing you to fit this transformative procedure easily into your busy life.

Thermage Treatment Process

The Thermage treatment process at KKC Clinic begins with a personalized consultation. Our team takes the time to understand your aesthetic goals and assess the areas you wish to improve. This initial discussion is crucial in crafting a treatment plan that aligns with your expectations and desired outcomes.
The Thermage FLX treatment itself is a seamless and comfortable experience. The procedure involves the application of a handheld device that delivers controlled energy to the deep layers of your skin.
Thanks to the advancements in the Thermage FLX system, patients experience minimal discomfort. The treatment is characterized by a sensation of warmth, which is managed effectively through the system’s integrated cooling feature. This ensures the procedure is not only effective but also comfortable from start to finish.
A typical Thermage session can last 30 to 90 minutes, depending on the treatment area’s size and complexity.
